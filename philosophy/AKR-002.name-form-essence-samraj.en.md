# Name–Form–Essence: Samraj, OCAM, and the Ontology of Naming

## 1. Name as Ontic Initiation

In OCAM, **Name** is the initiating act of being.  
It marks the separation of a phenomenon from the undifferentiated field of potentiality.

Name is **creative**, not descriptive.  
It is a call into existence.

---

## 2. Ontological Triad

The foundational triad is:

**Name → Form → Essence**

- Name individuates  
- Form configures  
- Essence reveals  

Each stage unfolds the previous one, transforming potential into structure and structure into meaning.

---

## 3. Acceptance as Reverse Vector

Naming moves **outward**:
- from energy  
- to structure  

Acceptance moves **inward**:
- from structure  
- to meaning  

Together they form a complete ontic cycle.

---

## 4. Samraj’s Contribution

The philosopher Samraj distinguishes:
- naming (affirming being)  
- meaning (revealing knowing)

OCAM extends this insight:

> **Naming creates being; meaning completes being.**

Thus OCAM shows that naming is not linguistic but **ontological**.

---

## 5. Punctum Initiatum

The *punctum initiatum* is the moment the inner impulse becomes Form through Name.  
At this point:

- transformation occurs  
- integration begins  
- the ontic trajectory unfolds  

This is the “first spark” of ontic differentiation.
